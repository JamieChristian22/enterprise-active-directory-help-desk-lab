# Operations Handbook

## Daily
- Review P1/P2 alerts and overnight incidents.
- Confirm backup success and investigate failed jobs.
- Check domain controller, DNS, DHCP, VPN, and internet health.
- Review disk capacity, CPU, memory, and failed services.
- Triage unassigned and aging tickets.
- Validate monitoring agent coverage.

## Weekly
- Review privileged sign-ins and failed authentication trends.
- Check certificate expiration and software update status.
- Review capacity growth and cloud spend anomalies.
- Test one documented runbook.
- Reconcile assets with endpoint management records.

## Monthly
- Patch servers in approved maintenance windows.
- Review SLA, availability, backup, and patch metrics.
- Test a representative backup restore.
- Review dormant accounts and privileged group membership.
- Update known errors, risks, and technical debt.

## Escalation
- P1: page on-call engineer and incident manager immediately.
- P2: assign primary engineer within 30 minutes.
- P3: standard queue; resolve or plan within two business days.
- P4: service request or low-impact issue; complete within five business days.
